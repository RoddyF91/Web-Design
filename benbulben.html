Benbulben

About Benbulben

Benbulben is one of Ireland's most iconic mountains, rising dramatically from the plains of County Sligo to a height of 526 metres. Its flat top and sheer limestone cliffs give it a distinctive table-top shape unlike any other mountain in Ireland, earning it the nickname Sligo's Table Mountain.

Formed over 320 million years ago when the area lay beneath a tropical sea, Benbulben was later carved into its current shape by glaciers during the last Ice Age. The mountain is home to rare Arctic alpine plants that have survived here since the glaciers retreated.

Mythology and Literature

Benbulben is deeply connected to Irish mythology. It is the legendary site where the hero Diarmuid met his fate on its slopes, gored by a great boar. The mountain also inspired the poet W.B. Yeats, who is buried nearby at Drumcliff Churchyard in its shadow. His epitaph reads: "Cast a cold eye on life, on death. Horseman, pass by."

What to See and Do

The Benbulben Forest Walk is a 5.5km loop beneath the mountain face, taking around 90 minutes and suitable for most fitness levels. More experienced walkers can take the full summit route, a 4 to 5 hour loop with views stretching across Donegal Bay on clear days.

Walking Routes

  1. Benbulben Forest Walk : 5.5km, 90 minutes, easy to moderate
  2. Kings Mountain Loop : 12km, 3 to 4 hours, moderate
  3. Full Summit Route : 15km, 4 to 5 hours, challenging

Good to Know

  • Height: 526 metres
  • Walk difficulty: Moderate to challenging
  • Nearest town: Sligo (12km south)
  • Facilities: Car park at the base

Getting There

Take the N15 from Sligo town heading north. After approximately 10km, follow the signposted turn to the right. A narrow road leads uphill to the car park directly beneath the mountain face.
