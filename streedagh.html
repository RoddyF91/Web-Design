Streedagh Beach

About Streedagh Beach

Streedagh Beach is a stunning three-kilometre stretch of wild Atlantic coastline in north County Sligo. Backed by sand dunes with views of Benbulben to the east and Inishmurray Island to the west, it is one of the most dramatic and unspoilt beaches in Ireland.

The Spanish Armada

The beach is famous for its connection to the Spanish Armada of 1588. Three Spanish ships were wrecked at Streedagh Point during a violent Atlantic storm, and over 1,100 men were reported lost on the strand. One survivor, Captain Francisco de Cuéllar, escaped and wrote a remarkable account of his time in Ireland. The wreck sites are protected as underwater archaeological monuments.

What to See and Do

Streedagh is a Blue Flag beach popular with swimmers, surfers and fossil hunters. Walking the full length of the strand and exploring the dunes takes one to two hours. At low tide, look for fossil corals and shells in the flat rocks at the water's edge.

Things to Do at Streedagh

  1. Walk the full 3km length of the strand
  2. Search for fossils in the rocks at low tide
  3. Explore the Sand Age monuments in the dunes
  4. Watch the sunset over Inishmurray Island

Good to Know

  • Beach length: 3km
  • Blue Flag status: Yes
  • Nearest town: Grange (2km)
  • Facilities: Car park, dog friendly

Getting There

From Sligo town, take the N15 north towards Grange. Follow the signs for Streedagh from Grange village. The beach car park is at the end of a narrow road through the dunes.
