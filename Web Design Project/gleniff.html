Gleniff Horseshoe

About the Gleniff Horseshoe

The Gleniff Horseshoe is a spectacular glacial valley on the northern slopes of the Dartry Mountains in north County Sligo. A 9km loop of narrow road winds through the valley, surrounded on three sides by towering limestone cliffs and peaks including Tievebaun, Truskmore, Benbulben, and Benwiskin.

The valley was carved by glaciers during the last Ice Age, when sheets of ice more than a kilometre thick covered the landscape. As the glaciers retreated northward, they gouged this enormous hollow from the mountain, leaving behind the fertile plain of Carbury below.

The Gleniff valley has strong connections to Irish mythology. High on the slopes above is a cave known as Diarmuid and Gráinne's Cave, where the legendary lovers are said to have hidden during their flight from the warrior Fionn Mac Cumhaill. The valley is also famous for the Magic Hill, an optical illusion where cars appear to roll uphill due to the surrounding terrain.

What to See and Do

The Gleniff Horseshoe Drive takes approximately 30 minutes by car and can also be done by bicycle or on foot. Look out for a small waterfall along the route, the ruins of an old barytes mill, and red squirrels in the forest sections. The full walking loop takes around two hours.

Good to Know

  • Loop distance: 9km by road
  • Walk difficulty: Easy to moderate
  • Nearest town: Cliffoney (6km)
  • Facilities: Lay-bys along the route

Getting There

From Sligo, take the N15 north for approximately 22km to Cliffoney village. Continue north for 2km to the first crossroads and turn right, following the signs for Gleniff. The loop road begins 6km along this road.
