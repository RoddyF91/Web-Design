Creevykeel Court Tomb

About Creevykeel

Creevykeel Court Tomb is one of the finest and best-preserved Neolithic monuments in Ireland, dating to between 4000 and 3500 BC. Located just off the N15 near Cliffoney village, it sits in an open field with a dramatic backdrop of Benbulben and the Atlantic coastline.

The tomb consists of a large oval-shaped ceremonial court open to the sky, leading into a covered stone gallery with two burial chambers. The entire structure measures approximately 55 metres in length. In its time the court would have been used for ritual ceremonies and the burial of the dead, serving the farming communities of Neolithic Sligo over many generations.

In 1935, the Harvard Archaeological Mission carried out one of the first modern scientific excavations in Ireland here. They uncovered cremated human remains, polished stone axes, and pottery fragments. Evidence of iron smelting was also found, showing the site continued to be used during the Early Christian period, centuries after its original construction.

What to See and Do

Creevykeel is freely accessible and managed by the Office of Public Works. A short walk of just a few metres from the roadside brings you directly into the forecourt of the tomb. Interpretive signage on site explains the history. The visit takes around 20 to 30 minutes and is suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

Good to Know

  • Age: Approximately 5,500 years old
  • Entry: Free, open all year
  • Nearest town: Cliffoney (1km)
  • Facilities: Roadside parking

Getting There

Creevykeel is located directly on the N15 between Sligo and Bundoran, approximately 25km north of Sligo town. Watch for the signpost and car park on the left-hand side of the road near Cliffoney village.
