Gleniff Horseshoe

About the Gleniff Horseshoe

The Gleniff Horseshoe is one of the most spectacular and secluded valleys in County Sligo, tucked into the Dartry Mountains between Benbulben and Truskmore. The valley takes its name from its distinctive horseshoe shape, formed when ancient glaciers carved a deep curved hollow out of the limestone plateau during the last Ice Age.

Sheer cliffs rise on three sides of the valley, reaching heights of over 300 metres and draped in cascading waterfalls after heavy rain. The floor of the horseshoe is a patchwork of farms and fields, making the contrast between the peaceful valley floor and the dramatic cliffs above all the more striking.

The valley is also connected to Irish mythology. According to local legend, Diarmuid and Grinne hid here during their flight from Fionn Mac Cumhaill, sheltering in a cave said to be visible on the cliff face. A small marker on the valley road identifies the traditional location of the cave.

The Scenic Drive

A narrow loop road winds around the valley floor and gives access to some breathtaking viewpoints. The circuit is approximately 8km and can be driven slowly or walked. The road climbs steeply at the far end of the horseshoe, offering views back down the valley and across to Benbulben and the Atlantic coastline in the distance.

What to See and Do

The valley is a quiet escape from the more popular tourist routes nearby. Walking the loop road gives the best chance to spot wildlife including red kite, peregrine falcon and red squirrel in the surrounding woodland. The cliffs are a popular destination for experienced climbers, with a number of routes graded across the limestone faces.

Highlights

  1. The horseshoe cliffs : towering limestone walls on three sides of the valley
  2. Waterfall views : streams cascade from the cliffs after rainfall
  3. Diarmuid and Grainne's Cave : marked cave linked to Irish mythology
  4. Valley loop walk : 8km circuit with outstanding views
  5. Wildlife spotting : red kite, peregrine falcon and red squirrel

Good to Know

  • Road type: Narrow single-track lane, drive slowly
  • Loop distance: Approx. 8km by road or on foot
  • Facilities: No visitor centre; small layby parking on the loop road
  • Best visited: Year-round, but most dramatic after heavy rain
  • Nearest town: Grange (5km south-west)
  • Distance from Sligo: Approx. 20km north on the N15

Getting There

From Sligo town, take the N15 north towards Bundoran. After approximately 15km, pass through Drumcliff and continue to the village of Grange. Just north of Grange, look for signs for the Gleniff Horseshoe and turn right onto the R279. Follow the narrow road into the valley. The entrance to the horseshoe loop is signposted.
