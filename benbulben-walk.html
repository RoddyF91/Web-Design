Benbulben Forest Walk

About the Walk

The Benbulben Forest Walk, also known as the Gortarowey Looped Walk, is a 5.5km trail that skirts the base of Benbulben mountain through Gortarowey Forest. It is widely considered one of the finest walks in Sligo, offering up-close and unrivalled views of Benbulben's iconic limestone cliffs without the need to climb the mountain itself.

The walk is managed jointly by Coillte and Sligo County Council and is freely accessible to the public year round. It is graded as easy, making it suitable for families, older visitors, and those with a moderate level of fitness. The terrain is mainly gravel forest paths with one gentle section of ascent along the northern face of the mountain.

Along the route, the trail passes through sections of mixed woodland including native oaks, opens out to sweeping views of Donegal Bay, Sligo Bay and the Atlantic coast, and passes the Cashelbaun ringfort, an early medieval stone enclosure dating from between 400 and 1100 AD. On a clear day, Slieve League in Donegal, the Ox Mountains and Knocknarea are all visible.

What to See Along the Route

The highlight of the walk comes at the northern corner of the loop, where the trail opens up directly beneath the sheer limestone cliffs of Benbulben. Looking up from here, the scale of the mountain is overwhelming. The views to the west take in the Atlantic coast, Inishmurray Island and Classiebawn Castle at Mullaghmore. Wildlife commonly spotted along the route includes wild hares, foxes, choughs, and sheep grazing on the steep upper slopes.

Points of Interest on the Walk

  1. Forest trail through native oak and mixed woodland
  2. Close-up views of Benbulben's limestone cliff face
  3. Panoramic views of Donegal Bay and the Atlantic coast
  4. Cashelbaun ringfort : early medieval stone enclosure
  5. Views of Inishmurray Island and Classiebawn Castle

Good to Know

  • Distance: 5.5km loop
  • Time: Approximately 1.5 hours
  • Grade: Easy
  • Terrain: Gravel forest path and open trail
  • Entry: Free, open all year
  • Facilities: Free car park at trailhead

What to Bring

Sturdy walking or hiking shoes are recommended as some sections can be muddy in wet weather. Dress in layers as conditions can change quickly. Bring water, a snack and a fully charged phone. Dogs are welcome but must be kept on a lead throughout.

Getting There

From Sligo town, take the N15 north for approximately 10km. Continue for 1.6km past Drumcliff village and take the right-hand turn signposted Barnaribbon, beside a thatched cottage. Follow the road straight ahead, keeping left where it veers right, until you reach the car park on your left directly beneath Benbulben.
